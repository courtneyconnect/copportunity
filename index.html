Buying Tips

Physical copper comes in bars, rounds, and bullion. It's heavy and has high shipping costs, so factor that in. Most buyers start with 1lb bars or rounds.

Spot price vs. premium: You'll always pay above the "spot price" (the live market price). The extra cost is called the premium √¢¬Ä¬î it covers minting, shipping, and dealer profit. Expect 20√¢¬Ä¬ì80% premiums on small copper purchases.

Paper copper: If you want exposure without holding physical metal, ETFs, futures, and mining stocks are options. These track the price more closely but you don't own actual copper.
